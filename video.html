Analysis of the use of video on the web

Studio Illicit

Studio-Illicit
I chose to analyse Studio Ilicit as an example to avoid and aim to steer clear of the design elements employed by the studio. While interactive and dynamic features can enhance the appeal of a website, they can also lead to cognitive overload. Studio Illicit presents an excellent example of how users can be distracted. While the experience is certainly visually rich, the combination of a moving video and dynamic typography creates an overloaded user experience. The video's length is suitable for an on-the-go experience, and it does enhance visual appeal—but the purpose does not go beyond that. The video does not contribute any additional meaning to the website's content which may place users in a situation where it is challenging to concentrate due to the abundance of moving elements. It is also worth noting that the video is presented in monochrome, with dark typography which creates a readability challenge due to low contrast.

Move Animation Studio

I chose Move Animation Studio as the visual design elements match my moodboard. The website is for an animation studio, and the videos are used to enhance appeal and information retention. The video is not on autoplay, which helps with not overloading the user with information. Videos are used, when necessary, as a sample of the studio’s projects. However, that was not the only way to deliver the information and the videos were not on autoplay mode. This is appropriate because it gives the user the option to watch the video which can cater to different styles while avoiding information overload. As it is not on autoplay and entirely loaded, this also helps with the issue of loading time.

Video Feature: Embedding TiktTok Article

A feature I have noticed in relation to videos is embedding TikTok videos. TikTok's short-form videos can serve as engaging and accessible content, catering to a short attention span. The platform's popularity among my chosen demographic (Gen Z) ensures that the content is relevant and relatable, potentially enhancing student interest. If I were to utilize the text content for medical resources and TikTok's for personable content, I can strike a balance. However, this will require evaluation of different content on TikTok as misinformation is prevalent. Striking a balance between the engaging nature of TikTok and maintaining a conducive learning environment would be the goal.

Selected Video Samples

Engaging in self-care by blowing out a candle adds a personal touch to the concept of wellness. It represents taking a moment for oneself, emphasizing the importance of mindful rituals and self-care routines. The act of blowing out a candle also represents letting go of stress and tension, contributing to an appealing representation of well-being.

Visualizing yoga and meditation can provide a concrete representation of wellness practices. Whether through images of serene yoga poses or videos of guided meditation, these elements can evoke a sense of tranquillity and mindfulness. They also serve as practical demonstrations of a hobby.

Users may relate with the depicted stress, recognizing it as something they may have experienced. This element serves as a contrast to the relaxation-focused content, highlighting the need for wellness practices. It can also act as a motivator, encouraging viewers to explore the presented wellness techniques as potential solutions to stress and anxiety that they might be facing.

The video of the sea aims to evoke a relaxing atmosphere. The movement of water can create a calming effect for the users. This multimedia element serves as a representation of the desired outcome of wellness practices – a state of trust and peace. It also can serve as a visual comma, offering users to pause and reset.

Reflection Point

While videos are popular content formats, they're not always used extensively on websites. On my site, I aim to minimise video use due to concerns about information overload. Like audio, videos have limitations: they are linear and time-dependent. Videos also require the users to engage fully, making them less versatile than other media. Additionally, large file sizes and long load times can hinder the user experience. In the videos I chose, MP4 was the file format I went for. I have not considered using any other file format because MP4 is a supported HTML5 video format and it maintains high video quality even after compression. I have compressed the videos to allow for faster loading time using Free Conver If not carefully considered, videos may disrupt the narrative and pacing of a webpage, detracting from its overall effectiveness. It is also worth mentioning that many wellness websites share this approach, prioritising concise textual content and images for easier consumption.
