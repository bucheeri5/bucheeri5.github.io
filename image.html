The Creative Process
Preperation It has been time consuming to build my background information on this project. This is because I was dealing with e-health articles and psychology. I delved into the topic, and it is especially important to have an accurate understanding following my analysis on text and narrative. It was very stimulating and inspiring to do research on web technologies and e-health, particularly psychology. I have found myself fully immersed in academic sources (mainly journal articles) to get an overview for text content. This has distraced me from immersing myself from engaging with different multimedia outputs to foster my creative thinking. This has taken on more significance as I have come to realise how important it is to communicate and display information effectively; otherwise, it is useless on its own. I started looking at sample portfolios and websites like CSS Zen Garden in an attempt to overcome my creative block and reduce the pressure on myself to complete a project quickly.
Incubation I have been paying close attention to e-health and wellness websites. Aside from analysing the websites in this portfolio, I try to mentally note what works well and what could be improved (for example, text-to-image ratios). Along the way, I tried to experiment with design, which is why I had multiple CSS files during this journey, and I incorporated them to see how different designs fit my portfolio. In terms of taking mental notes in my surroundings, the incubation process has been extremely effective. One example is my experience with the moodle website. While the side bar is not as helpful for navigation, being able to expand and collapse sections is, especially on long pages.
Ideation My "aha" moment was tough to come by, notably at first when I was discouraged. Stepping away from the project and focusing on other assessments gave my subconscious mind time to work on led me to breakthroughs. Another way that helped me develop my ideas was employing mind maps helped me overcome mental and creative blocks. This was when i was able to make connections based on the position my audience are in. For example, i thought of students societies and welfare officers—i was able to connect that to my idea through framing this resource as one that welfare officers can utilitze for wellbeing related events wihtin student societies. Collaboration was another beneficial process. This is critical when it comes to design thinking and UX/UI design and being innovative. While being creative can be achieved individually, innovation can be fostered through collaborative thinking and basic interactions with my peers. Aside from working with my peer support group, I have easy access to my intended audience. My audience analysis page contains an example of audience analysis that I found valuable.
Evaluation I find myself constantly evaluating my idea, particularly my topic and its creativity. I've come to believe that it's worthwhile to pursue. This was confirmed following my consultation, and while I have been going through doubts as this idea formed, I am incorporating feedback to improve my understanding of what I should pursue. I initially had difficulty distinguishing between design thinking and self-doubt. My constant evaluation of various websites has also been beneficial in supporting my analysis and evaluation. This helped my learning curve, and at times when evaluating websites for features like audio, I took mental notes for inspiration and incorporated them into my moodboard.
Implementation My portfolio is evolving and will keep evolving to reflect my creative journey. This page describes how my ideas evolved over time and how this portfolio came to be. While I continued to incorporate new items, insights from the AWE process and my analysis of various projects influenced my choices hence leading to constant upgrades. I believe my growth is reflected in how I changed the various things I implemented through research, feedback from my peer support groups, and immersing myself in the creative process, especially through experimentation. My portfolio has not been static, and I find myself going back and forth on different pages and adjusting. The criteria for including items varied based on trends, especially when using tools such as Voyant. Because the implementation was ongoing, it was a good information management practice to keep my GitHub page for backups up to date.
Use of Images in the Web

Youngminds

Images on the Youngminds website are mainly raster images. They are mainly used in reference to other pages, such as blogs or differentating who are the users—sections for parents have older people, and mental health resources for young people have pictures of adolscents. This has been helpful in terms of navigational aid and allowing users to identify the content or context associated with a particular link or section. Another feature with images to note is the consistency in style and quality of images which made the website appear to be cohesive.

NHSInform

The NHSInform does not make extensive use of images. In some cases, they are at the top of the page or serve as a link to blog-style articles. The images used are raster images with diverse representation. In comparison to other websites, it relies heavily on text. This might have been because it is an official website primarily for textual information. However, in some cases, such as skin condition pages, the text lacks any accompanying images. If included, this can elevate the content and assist the website in attaining its goal of informing people about their health. Because raster images can be large, it is worth noting that they appear to have been optimised in this website, enabling reasonable page loading times.

HH Manifesto

One of my primary design influences is the HH Manifesto. The images flow very smoothly and complement the website, and they are all vectors. Images are used with purpose; for example, when statistics about mental health are presented, they are accompanied by small vectors that aid in retention (especially when large typography and short sentences are used.) The images flow smoothly, implying a well-thought-out design strategy. Notable is the careful consideration given to image placement, sizing, and alignment in order to create a unified visual narrative as users navigate through the content.
