Multimedia: Audio

Analysis of the use of audio on the web

Supershine

Supershine is a web development studio, and I selected it as an inspiration for the art style I want to achieve with my website. Users can choose whether to listen to the audio on the homepage, and they can pause it at any time. This is a useful feature for avoiding intrusive autoplay. The audio kicks off as a speech that enhances the onboarding experience and contributes to storytelling. The speech is transcribed, which improves accessibility. Following the transition, users are directed to the main homepage, where they can listen to music while navigating the website's content. While the audio does not aid in information retention, it is an effective marketing tool for increasing appeal and demonstrating what services the studio can provide as a web development service.

Suitable Audio Samples

I curated audio samples that I might consider for my website. I chose audio samples with slower tempo and less variance in instruments to avoid causing sensory overload to the users. Keywords like "cheerful" and "motivating" seem to be the most appropriate. I also explored terms like "dreamy," "ambient," "motivational," and "inspirational” which match my website’s narrative. If I proceed with incorporating audio in this project, I believe a side menu down to play the music samples would be most appropriate; similar to the drop-menu on supershine. This is done to avoid having intrusive autoplay.

Analysis

Stereo is better sounding and may enhance the user experience, which allows for a more dynamic and spatial representation of sound. The stereo with the quality of 170-210 Kbps is the better sounding audio, but the difference in quality is minimal and quite hard to distinguish—in my situation, I had to increase the volume and listen in on my headphones instead of my computer’s speaker. This makes the stereo 145-185 Kbps option the most appropriate one to choose, if implemented in my proposed website.

Reflection Point

I recognise that my website will contain a variety of multimedia elements, and the design I want to create is intended to be relaxing while also acknowledging that users are likely stressed. Unlike images and text, which users process at their own pace, audio is linear, time-sensitive, and transient. This makes it an element that I deem less suitable for use in this project.
