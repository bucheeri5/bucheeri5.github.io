What is e-health literacy?

E-health literacy combines the skills of traditional literacy, health literacy, and information technology. It empowers individuals to seek, find, understand, and appraise health information from electronic sources and apply the knowledge gained to addressing or solving a health problem.

As digital health resources become more prevalent, understanding how to navigate these tools effectively is essential for patients and healthcare providers alike.

Navigating Information Online

In the digital age, the ability to navigate and assess the reliability of online health information is crucial. Misinformation can spread widely, making it important to develop skills for identifying credible sources and verifying the accuracy of health-related content.

This skillset includes evaluating the authority of websites, understanding the purpose of the information presented, and checking for references or evidence that supports the content.

Protecting Health Information

With the increasing amount of personal health information being stored and shared online, it's vital to adopt measures that ensure data security and privacy. This includes being aware of how health data is collected, stored, and used by various platforms and services.

Implementing strong passwords, understanding privacy settings, and knowing your rights under health information privacy laws are key steps in protecting your health data online.
