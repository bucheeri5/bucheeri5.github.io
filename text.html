Professional writing characteristics & Evaluating the YoungMinds Website

Accuracy The information is not cited, and while it is just a charity and may raise less concern over commercial interests, it does not signpost to the resources used for advice. This raises questions about the ethos of the YoungMinds. Positive Voice The content is presented in a positive voice with heavy use of first and second person pronouns.
Active Voice The website uses an active voice to engage users, with a lot of first and second person pronouns. As a user this choice makes the website seem much more personable and less authoritative. Strong Nouns and Verbs The use of strong nouns and verbs is especially evident in the section targeting parents, and sections advising to act upon the situations.
Biases The website presents balanced information and refers to what the NHS does and their process. It is local to the United Kingdom so users from other parts of the world may benefit less from certain sections, such as local services. Parallelism There is evidence of parallelism in the website. This creates a sense of balance and symmetry in the sentences. For example— “I try to move away from overwhelming situations, but I actively avoid being alone if I don’t trust myself to stay safe. Even if in the moment I don’t feel like I can talk, I try to speak to someone once I feel calmer and always take it slowly as I recover”
Clarity The information is presented in a clear and understandable manner with signposting when needed for further information (such as NHS). Sentence Variety Sentence structure varies very differently across the website. For example, some articles are spoken in first person and are shorter. Advice sections had lengthier sentences. This reflects the nature of the content.
Conciseness The content is concise and to the point, avoiding unnecessary details. However, in blogs, because it is more personable it is more conversational and personable so less concise. Tone and Voice The tone and voice are appropriate for a website that targets young people as it is encouraging and helpful.
Conversational & Smoothly Flowing Prose Contractions are present across the website. The vocabulary used is simple for a broad audience. The structure flows smoothly, mainly in articles that focus on personal experiences. Main pages are less conversational. Simple Words Simple language and vocabulary is used, which makes the information very accessible especially to young audiences.
Correctness The website content seems to be proofread; no grammar or spelling errors spotted. Shorter Paragraphs The information is broken into shorter paragraphs which enables easier reading, except for the strategy pages and blogs that are information dense and talk about mental health procedures and services.

Voyant-tools

My analysis of the professional writing characteristics was mainly based on my own judgment and standard, and in terms of evaluation it was qualitative. When I integrated Voyant-tool, I have come to appreciate the value of incorporating findings from statistical data as my biases hindered some of my judgement-such as how dense the vocabulary is. This allowed me to analyse from a more data driven perspective. By analysing multiple websites through the Voyant-tool, I come to see the patterns in mental health related websites. Relying on the insights of numbers such as the high frequency words is helping me in my design thinking and making informed decisions about the language to use.I chose websites that align with my topic; youngminds (What is Mental Health?) and NHSInform(Mental Wellbeing) Key takeaways of trends:

Vocabulary density:

  • Highest: NHS (0.278)
  • Young Minds (0.274)
The vocabulary density of NHS is 0.278, and Young Minds is 0.274. This indicates that a significant portion of their content consists of unique words (approximately 1 in 5 words is unique), suggesting a rich and diverse vocabulary in their materials. This could be beneficial for econveying nuanced information effectively. However, a dense vocabulary might make the content more challenging to grasp if the words are advanced in nature.

Average Words Per Sentence:

  • Highest: Mental wellbeing NHS (40.1)
  • What is Mental Health? … (26.2)
The average words per sentence in the NHSInform website is 40.1, while What is Mental Health? By YoungMinds is 26.2. Longer sentences, as seen in the NHS content, might provide more in-depth information but could potentially be more challenging for some readers. Shorter sentences, as seen in YoungMinds, may enhance readability but might lack the necessary depth.

Readability Index:

  • Mental wellbeing NHS (38.810)
  • What is Mental Health? (36.000)
A higher index generally = more complex language. While the difference here is relatively small, it implies that the NHS content might be slightly more advanced in terms of language complexity.

Most frequent words in the corpus:

  • mental (73); help (70); health (66); guide (39); support (38)
The most frequently used words in the corpus reflect key themes related to mental health and support, indicating a focus on these aspects in the content.

Reflection Point

Text is the backbone of a website, no matter how much the visual design itself is polished. Its clarity and readability directly impact user experience and accessibility. This influences navigation and understanding. Beyond its functional role, text contributes to brand identity through consistent tone, messaging, and the establishment of a brand's voice. Text enhances discoverability when it comes to SEO and persuasive language can drive user engagement. In the dynamic landscape of responsive design, thoughtful handling of text ensures adaptability across various devices. In the proposed website, I will be conducting further research to be able to go into depth with the topic while still ensuring that it is comprehensive.
