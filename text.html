Professional writing characteristics & Evaluating the YoungMinds Website
Accuracy The information is not cited, and while it is just a charity it does not signpost to the resources they used. Positive Voice The content is presented in a positive voice, especially with heavy use of first and second person pronouns.
Active Voice The website uses an active voice to engage users, with a lot of first and second person pronouns. As a user this choice makes the website seem much more personable and less authoriative. Strong Nouns and Verbs The use of strong nouns and verbs is especially evident in the section targetting parents.
Biases The website presents balanced information, however it is very local to the United Kingdom. Parallelism This is consistent in the website.
Clarity The information is presented in a clear and understandable manner. Sentence Variety Sentence structure varies very differently across the website.
Conciseness The content is concise and to the point, avoiding unnecessary details. Tone and Voice The tone and voice are appropriate for healthcare information.
Conversational & Smoothly Flowing Prose The content flows smoothly and feels conversational. Simple Words Simple language is used, which makes the information very accessible especially to young audiences.
Correctness The website content is free from errors and inaccuracies. Shorter Paragraphs The information is broken into shorter paragraphs which enables easier reading, except for the strategy pages and blogs which are expected to be text heavy.
The Awe writing process
Assess As I am framing the narrative and building on the text, I am focusing is on Glasgow University students, ensuring resonance with their specific needs. The primary goal is clear—to inform and support student in managing their mental health. My initial research heavily drew on academic sources. As this project unfolds, I plan to go beyond that and integrate interviews for a qualitative perspective. This is a conscious effort of design thinking—embracing the evolving insights of the journey. conscious effort to integrate authentic voices and relate to the lived experiences of my audience.
Write Outline of writing will be: broken down sentences, active voice, and conciness.
I am aware of what the general writing style I want but it is still shaping into what I specifically want.
Edit Reflecting on editing, as I am going through the process of drafting the porfoltio I realize how I need to go back and re-evaluate my work. Aside from proofreading, upon constant research my understanding of things naturally improves; this is why my website needs to reflect that dynamic.
Voyant-tools

My analysis of the professional writing characteristics was mainly based on my own judgment and standard, and in terms of evaluation it was qualitative. When I integrated Voyant-tool, I have come to appreciate the value of incorporating findings from statistical data. This allowed me to analyse from a more data driven perspective. By analysing multiple websites through the voyant tools, I come to see the patterns in mental health related websites. Relying on the insights of numbers such as the high frequency words is helping me in my design thinking and making informed decisions about the language I am using.

Reflection Point

Text is the backbone of a website, no matter how much visual design is important. Its clarity and readability directly impact user experience and accessibility, influencing navigation and understanding. Beyond its functional role, text contributes to brand identity through consistent tone, messaging, and the establishment of a brand's voice. Optimized for search engines, text enhances discoverability, while well-crafted calls to action leverage persuasive language to drive user engagement. In the dynamic landscape of responsive design, thoughtful handling of text ensures adaptability across various devices.
